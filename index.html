Annotations of Early Draft of Declaration of Independence

"The act for opening free ports in the West-Indies declares Negroes a lawful commodity
and to be imported and sold there upon paying a certain duty; but neither upon the passing
that act, nor upon the passing any former one for the encouragement of the African trade, does
there appear a single protest in abhorrence of that trade, or of treating rational creatures as property."




"[It] was the case in some parts of Britain not many years since, to transfer the service of the
inhabitants and the power over their lives, with the title to the lands; and it was the universal
practice among the Asiatic nations, the Jews on excepted, to consider a man's wife and children
as his effects, and to sell them for the satisfaction of his creditors, as appears from all history
ancient and modern, but most authentically from the parable of debtor and creditor, recorded by St. Matthew."




"The Negroes being arrived in our colonies, are put up to sale by their owners, and the planters purchase
them. The Fantees are generally carried to Jamiaca, where they are greatly valued on account of their
hardiness and high spirit. But as they were freemen in their own country, they are very difficult to be
managed as slaves, and this is the principal reason of the frequent insurrection in that island; while upon
the continent especially, insurrections are seldom heard of."
